# Code of Conduct

Crudspa is an engineering project. Strong technical disagreement is welcome. Contempt, harassment, and bad-faith behavior are not.

We want this repository to feel like a room full of experienced teammates: direct, respectful, and focused on making the software better.

## Expected Behavior

* Be respectful, professional, and technically honest.
* Critique ideas, code, and tradeoffs without attacking people.
* Assume good intent until there is evidence otherwise.
* Make room for developers with different levels of experience.
* Keep discussions relevant to the project and useful for future readers.
* Respect privacy, licenses, and confidential information shared for troubleshooting.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in project spaces:

* Harassment, discrimination, threats, or intimidation.
* Personal attacks, insults, or repeated hostile behavior.
* Trolling, dogpiling, or bad-faith argument meant to wear people down.
* Doxxing or sharing private information without permission.
* Sexualized language or imagery in project spaces.
* Retaliation against someone who reports a concern in good faith.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

* GitHub issues, pull requests, and discussions
* project email and other project-related communication
* public or private settings where someone is acting as a Crudspa maintainer or representative

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, report it by email:

* `jason@crudspa.org`
* `support@crudspa.org`

Please include links, screenshots, timestamps, and any other context that would help us review the report fairly.

If your report concerns one of those contacts, use the other address.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers may edit, remove, or reject content that violates this Code of Conduct. They may also pause or close conversations that are no longer productive.

## Enforcement

Depending on severity and pattern, maintainers may take actions including:

* Issuing a warning.
* Removing comments, commits, or other content.
* Temporarily restricting participation.
* Permanently banning someone from project spaces.

Reports will be reviewed as confidentially as practical.

Retaliation against someone who reports a concern or assists with a review is itself a violation of this Code of Conduct.

## Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was made in error, reply to the enforcement notice or email the alternate contact above. A different reviewer will handle the appeal when possible.
